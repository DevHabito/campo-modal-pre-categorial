# Exact Central-Mean Support-Size Family

**Programme:** Modal Field Research Programme  
**Provisional audit:** A67  
**Author line:** Felipe Gianini Romero  
**Status:** exact five-member support-size family theorem; not an arbitrary-\(M\) symbolic theorem

## Technical abstract

A66 proved exact global continuous first-anchor monotonicity for two fixed
contracts. A67 asks whether the canonical result survives changes in the
number of microscopic support states.

The audited family is

\[
\operatorname{supp}P\subseteq\{0,\ldots,M\},
\qquad
M\in\{5,6,7,8,9\},
\]

with

\[
\mathbb E[X]=\frac M2,
\qquad
\mu=1,
\qquad
\delta=\frac1{1875}.
\]

The absolute tolerance is the A64 scale-normalized value

\[
\varepsilon_M
=
\delta\,\ell_1\!\left(M,\frac M2\right).
\]

For each support, the A64-optimal completion containing the boundary pair is
held fixed:

\[
D_{\alpha,M}
=
\{\alpha,3,\gamma_M\},
\qquad
2\le\alpha<3,
\]

where

\[
\gamma_5=10,
\qquad
\gamma_6=5,
\qquad
\gamma_7=\gamma_8=\gamma_9=4.
\]

The exact parametric programmes decompose into:

\[
\boxed{33\text{ algebraic phases}}
\]

joined by

\[
\boxed{28\text{ simple algebraic transitions}}.
\]

For every phase:

- primal feasibility is certified;
- active dual multipliers are certified;
- nonbasic reduced costs are certified;
- inactive observation bands are certified;
- denominators remain finite;
- the derivative factor
  \[
  \kappa_M(s)
  =
  -s\frac{d\rho_M}{ds}
  \]
  is strictly positive.

The positive \(\alpha\)-band remains active in every one of the 33 phases.

All adjacent value branches agree at their algebraic transitions.
Consequently, for every declared support maximum,

\[
\boxed{
\frac{d\rho_M}{d\alpha}>0
\quad
\text{for all }2\le\alpha<3.
}
\]

Therefore:

\[
\boxed{
\alpha_M^\star=2
\qquad
(M=5,6,7,8,9)
}
\]

is the unique global first-anchor optimum for each fixed completion.

This is the first exact result in the programme showing that the
first-boundary law survives a nontrivial family of different microscopic
support sizes.

## 2. Exact theorem

### Theorem 2.1

For each

\[
M\in\{5,6,7,8,9\},
\]

let \(\rho_M(\alpha)\) be the exact direct-\(Q\) minimax ratio generated by

\[
D_{\alpha,M}
=
\{\alpha,3,\gamma_M\}
\]

under the declared central-mean normalized-noise contract.

Then \(\rho_M\) is continuous on

\[
[2,3)
\]

and strictly increasing.

Hence:

\[
\boxed{
\arg\min_{2\le\alpha<3}\rho_M(\alpha)=\{2\}.
}
\]

The same conclusion holds for

\[
Q_M(\alpha)
=
\frac12\log_2\rho_M(\alpha).
\]

### Proof structure

For every \(M\):

1. enumerate the exact active primal–dual phases;
2. express each phase value as a rational function of
   \[
   s=2^{-\alpha};
   \]
3. isolate every phase-transition root by a rational interval;
4. prove all primal, dual, reduced-cost, slack, and denominator signs;
5. prove
   \[
   -s\rho'_M(s)>0
   \]
   on each phase;
6. prove equality of adjacent value branches at every transition.

Since \(s\) decreases strictly with \(\alpha\), positivity of
\(-s\rho'_M(s)\) gives strict increase in \(\alpha\).

## 4. Common structure

The complete active-basis sequences differ between support sizes, but one
feature is invariant:

\[
\boxed{
\text{the positive first-anchor band is active in every phase.}
}
\]

This gives a concrete structural explanation for the boundary law.

The adversarial pair always uses the first observation at its positive error
boundary. Moving \(\alpha\) away from the target weakens precisely the
constraint that remains active throughout the entire minimax path.

Other observation bands do not possess the same universality:

- the \(\beta=3\) band may become inactive near coalescence;
- the \(\gamma_M\) band may become inactive;
- its active sign may switch from positive to negative;
- primal support states enter and leave in support-dependent patterns.

Thus the first anchor is structurally distinguished from the completion
anchors.

## 5. Growth with support size

The boundary risk increases with \(M\):

\[
Q_5(2)
<
Q_6(2)
<
Q_7(2)
<
Q_8(2)
<
Q_9(2).
\]

The coalescence limit also increases.

This does not mean that a larger physical system must be intrinsically worse.
Within the declared contract, increasing \(M\) enlarges the hidden
distributional freedom while retaining only three transform observations.

The result is therefore informational:

> more microscopic degrees of freedom, under a fixed observation budget,
> permit greater target ambiguity.

A separate experiment would be needed to distinguish support-size effects
from observation-budget effects.

## 6. What generalized

The following conclusion now holds exactly for five distinct supports:

\[
\boxed{
\alpha^\star=2.
}
\]

It survives changes in:

- microscopic dimension;
- normalized absolute tolerance;
- optimal third-anchor completion;
- number and positions of primal–dual phase transitions;
- active support geometry.

This substantially reduces the chance that the A66 canonical result was an
accident of the six-state support.

---

## 7. What did not become universal

A67 does not produce one closed symbolic formula valid for every integer
\(M\).

The five problems still require separate phase decompositions. In particular:

- phase counts differ;
- transition polynomials differ;
- active supports differ;
- \(\gamma_M\) changes between the supports;
- no induction in \(M\) has yet been proved.

Therefore the correct statement is:

\[
\boxed{
\text{an exact five-member support-size family theorem}
}
\]

—not—

\[
\text{a theorem for all finite support sizes}.
\]

## 8. Logical status

### Established exactly

1. Five complete global continuous minimax theorems.
2. Thirty-three exact algebraic phases.
3. Twenty-eight simple algebraic transitions.
4. Exact primal–dual optimality throughout every phase.
5. Strictly positive derivative throughout every phase.
6. Exact continuity at every transition.
7. Unique global boundary optimum for every \(M=5,\ldots,9\).
8. Positive first-anchor band active in every phase.

### Not established

1. A symbolic theorem for arbitrary \(M\).
2. An induction principle in support size.
3. Joint reoptimization of \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\) as \(\alpha\) varies.
4. Other means or target exponents.
5. Continuous microscopic support.
6. A fixed observation budget that grows with \(M\).

## 9. Next rigorous target

The next question should be structural rather than another finite extension:

> Why is the positive first-anchor band active in every phase?

A promising route is to derive the dual approximation problem explicitly.
The dual constructs upper and lower affine-exponential envelopes of the
target function using the observed exponentials.

The next audit should test whether total positivity implies that an optimal
dual envelope must touch the first admissible exponential with positive sign.

A successful dual-envelope theorem could replace the five separate phase
proofs with a single argument that applies to arbitrary support size.
