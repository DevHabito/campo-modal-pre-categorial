## Technical abstract

A45 derived the complete continuous-\(\gamma\) phase diagram at the single positive-noise
benchmark

\[
\varepsilon=10^{-4}.
\]

At that benchmark the direct minimax future-score risk is constant near the duplicated anchor
\(\gamma=3\), then decreases, and is minimized at the compactified boundary
\(\gamma=\infty\).

That conclusion must not be extrapolated to every \(\varepsilon>0\). The present audit
releases the noise level locally and derives the rigorous bifurcation skeleton in the
\((\varepsilon,\gamma)\) plane.

The contract remains

\[
\operatorname{supp}P\subseteq\{0,1,2,3,4,5\},
\qquad
\mathbb E[X]=\frac52,
\]

with anchors \(2\log2\) and \(3\log2\), third observation \(\gamma\log2\), common absolute
tolerance \(\varepsilon\), and direct future-score risk under the centered contraction
\(a=1/2\).

Set

\[
r=2^{-\gamma}\in[0,1/8].
\]

Four exact conclusions are obtained.

1. **Redundancy boundary.**  
   The duplicated-anchor minimax pair remains feasible until a boundary
   \(r_{\mathrm{red}}(\varepsilon)\). As \(\varepsilon\downarrow0\),

   \[
   r_{\mathrm{red}}(\varepsilon)
   =
   \frac18-\frac{63232}{735}\varepsilon+O(\varepsilon^2),
   \]

   so

   \[
   \gamma_{\mathrm{red}}(\varepsilon)
   =
   3+
   \frac{505856}{735\log2}\varepsilon
   +O(\varepsilon^2).
   \]

   The width of the redundant-parameter plateau is therefore linear in the error level.

2. **Regular finite local optimum.**  
   A distinct stationary branch exists for sufficiently small positive error. It is
   characterized by a rational equation

   \[
   \varepsilon=E_5(r),
   \]

   and is a strict local minimum. As \(\varepsilon\downarrow0\),

   \[
   r_5(\varepsilon)
   =
   \frac18
   -
   \frac{8\sqrt{174}}7\sqrt{\varepsilon}
   +
   \frac{19456}{147}\varepsilon
   +
   O(\varepsilon^{3/2}),
   \]

   hence

   \[
   \gamma_5(\varepsilon)
   =
   3+
   \frac{64\sqrt{174}}{7\log2}\sqrt{\varepsilon}
   +
   \frac{913408}{147\log2}\varepsilon
   +
   O(\varepsilon^{3/2}).
   \]

   Thus the robustly useful separation scales as \(\sqrt{\varepsilon}\), not
   \(\varepsilon\).

3. **Finite-branch annihilation.**  
   The regular branch and a boundary-active branch meet at an algebraic bifurcation

   \[
   \varepsilon_a
   \approx
   3.687849908801989\times10^{-5},
   \]

   \[
   r_a\approx0.03803800406316864,
   \qquad
   \gamma_a\approx4.716414642937835.
   \]

   The transition is generated by a double root of the phase-contact equation.

4. **Compactified-boundary threshold.**  
   The boundary-active stationary point reaches \(r=0\), equivalently
   \(\gamma=\infty\), at the exact rational error level

   \[
   \varepsilon_b
   =
   \frac{189}{2367604}
   \approx
   7.982753872691547\times10^{-5}.
   \]

   Below \(\varepsilon_b\), the compactified boundary is not a local minimum of that branch.
   Above \(\varepsilon_b\), it is.

Consequently, positive noise does not immediately force the best local design to
\(\gamma=\infty\). The local design topology is:

\[
0<\varepsilon<\varepsilon_a:
\quad
\text{finite regular local minimum},
\]

\[
\varepsilon_a<\varepsilon<\varepsilon_b:
\quad
\text{finite boundary-active local minimum},
\]

\[
\varepsilon>\varepsilon_b:
\quad
\gamma=\infty
\text{ is a local minimum}.
\]

At \(\varepsilon=10^{-4}>\varepsilon_b\), A45 additionally proves that the compactified
minimum is global. A46 does **not** claim that the local branches identified here constitute
the complete global two-dimensional phase diagram for every intermediate error level. That
stronger claim still requires exhaustive semi-algebraic certification of all optimal bases.

\[

The direct future-score width is

## 9. What A46 corrects—and what it does not

A45 remains correct for its declared benchmark

\[
\varepsilon=10^{-4}.
\]

The correction concerns extrapolation:

\[
\boxed{
\text{positive noise alone does not imply }
\gamma^\star=\infty.
}
\]

For sufficiently small positive noise there is a finite regular local minimum, and its
separation from the anchor follows a square-root law.

### Established

1. The redundancy plateau has linear small-noise width.
2. The regular stationary branch has a unique local minimum characterized by
   \(E_5(r)=\varepsilon\).
3. Its separation scales as \(\sqrt{\varepsilon}\).
4. Its risk has a \(\sqrt{\varepsilon}\) noise penalty.
5. The regular and boundary-active local branches meet at the algebraic threshold
   \(\varepsilon_a\).
6. The boundary-active stationary point reaches \(\gamma=\infty\) at the exact rational
   threshold \(\varepsilon_b\).
7. At \(\varepsilon=10^{-4}\), the A45 global compactified result lies above that threshold.

### Not established

1. A complete globally optimal partition of the entire
   \((\varepsilon,\gamma)\) rectangle has not yet been certified.
2. The local minimum identified for every small \(\varepsilon\) is not promoted here to a
   global theorem for the whole parameter domain.
3. No empirical noise model or covariance law is inferred.
4. No physical cost, support, anchor, or measurement parameter is calibrated.
5. The threshold values are contract-relative.
