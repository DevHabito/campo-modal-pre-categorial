## Technical abstract

A51 proved that the first target-excluding anchor is driven to the nearest
location allowed by the declared exclusion distance. The present audit asks a
different question: after fixing that first anchor at exponent \(2\), is the
second anchor \(3\) merely the best integer candidate, or is it a genuine
continuous optimum?

Two controlled contracts are studied on

\[
2\le\beta\le4.
\]

For exact data,

\[
D_0(\beta)=\{2,\beta,4\}\log2.
\]

For common absolute tolerance

\[
\varepsilon=10^{-4},
\]

\[
D_\varepsilon(\beta)=\{2,\beta,\infty\}\log2.
\]

The microscopic contract remains

\[
\operatorname{supp}P\subseteq\{0,1,2,3,4,5\},
\qquad
\mathbb E[X]=\frac52,
\]

and the target remains \(L(\log2)\).

Set

\[
s=2^{-\beta}.
\]

For every distinct exact-data design \(2<\beta<4\), the direct minimax ratio is

\[
\boxed{
\rho_0(\beta)
=
\frac{1036s+2063}
{2(539s+1021)}.
}
\]

Its derivative is

\[
\frac{d\rho_0}{ds}
=
-\frac{54201}
{2(539s+1021)^2}<0,
\]

so

\[
\frac{d\rho_0}{d\beta}>0.
\]

Therefore exact data prefer the second anchor arbitrarily close to the first:

\[
\boxed{
\inf_{2<\beta<4}\rho_0(\beta)
=
\frac{1548}{1541},
}
\]

attained only in the singular limit

\[
\beta\downarrow2.
\]

There is no exact minimizer on the closed interval. At either duplicated
endpoint, \(\beta=2\) or \(\beta=4\), the design collapses to the same two

At \(\varepsilon=10^{-4}\), the complete interval is covered by eight exact

## 7. Interpretation

The first and second anchors obey different rules.

### First anchor

The first anchor wants to approach the omitted target as closely as the
contract permits:

\[
\alpha^\star=1+\Delta.
\]

### Second anchor

With positive noise, the second anchor must balance two competing effects:

- moving closer to the first anchor provides local or derivative-like
  information;
- moving too close makes the difference vulnerable to noise.

This tradeoff produces a genuine interior optimum:

\[
\beta^\star\approx2.7284.
\]

With exact data, the noise penalty disappears and the second anchor again
tries to coalesce with the first.
