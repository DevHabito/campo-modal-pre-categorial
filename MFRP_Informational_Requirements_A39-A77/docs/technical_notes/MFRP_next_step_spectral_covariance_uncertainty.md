## Technical abstract

A60 treated the covariance matrix \(\Sigma\) as known exactly. The present audit
allows the covariance itself to be uncertain.

Let

\[
\widehat\Sigma\succ0
\]

be an estimated covariance matrix and let

\[
\tau\ge0
\]

be a certified spectral error radius. Define

\[
\mathcal S_\tau
=
\left\{
\Sigma=\Sigma^\top\succeq0:
\|\Sigma-\widehat\Sigma\|_2\le\tau
\right\}.
\]

For every \(\Sigma\in\mathcal S_\tau\),

\[
-\tau I
\preceq
\Sigma-\widehat\Sigma
\preceq
\tau I,
\]

hence

\[
\widehat\Sigma-\tau I
\preceq
\Sigma
\preceq
\widehat\Sigma+\tau I.
\]

A60 proved that the robust minimax risk is monotone in the Loewner order.
Therefore:

\[
\boxed{
\sup_{\Sigma\in\mathcal S_\tau}
Q^\star(\Sigma)
=
Q^\star(\widehat\Sigma+\tau I).
}
\]

The upper bound is attained because

\[
\widehat\Sigma+\tau I\in\mathcal S_\tau.
\]

If

\[
\tau<\lambda_{\min}(\widehat\Sigma),
\]

then

\[
\widehat\Sigma-\tau I\succ0
\]

also belongs to the uncertainty set, and the exact two-sided interval is

\[
\boxed{
Q^\star(\widehat\Sigma-\tau I)
\le
Q^\star(\Sigma)
\le
Q^\star(\widehat\Sigma+\tau I).
}
\]

Thus covariance-estimation uncertainty does not require a second nested
optimization. It is absorbed by isotropic spectral inflation.

For the audited nominal estimate

\[

\operatorname{diag}
\left(

\frac9{40000}
\right),

the smallest eigenvalue is

\[

=

=
0.000225,
\]

and the largest diagonal entry is \(9/10000\). The A58 containment condition
permits

\[
\boxed{

}
\]

The point-estimate robust risk is

## 1. Spectral uncertainty set

The operator norm condition

\[
\|\Sigma-\widehat\Sigma\|_2\le\tau
\]

is equivalent, for symmetric matrices, to

\[
-\tau I
\preceq
\Sigma-\widehat\Sigma
\preceq
\tau I.
\]

Therefore every admissible covariance lies between two Loewner endpoints.

This is stronger than checking the entries separately: it controls every
linear combination of channel errors.

---

## 2. Exact worst covariance theorem

Let

\[
\mathcal Q(\Sigma)
\]

denote the exact A60 robust future risk generated by covariance \(\Sigma\).

A60 established

\[
\Sigma_1\preceq\Sigma_2
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\mathcal Q(\Sigma_1)
\le
\mathcal Q(\Sigma_2).
\]

Since every covariance in the spectral ball satisfies

\[
\Sigma\preceq\widehat\Sigma+\tau I,
\]

we obtain

\[
\mathcal Q(\Sigma)
\le
\mathcal Q(\widehat\Sigma+\tau I).
\]

The inflated covariance is itself admissible because

\[
\|
(\widehat\Sigma+\tau I)
-
\widehat\Sigma
\|_2
=
\tau.
\]

Hence the upper bound is exact and attained.

---

## 3. Exact lower endpoint

If

\[
\tau<\lambda_{\min}(\widehat\Sigma),
\]

then

\[
\widehat\Sigma-\tau I\succ0.
\]

Every admissible covariance satisfies

\[
\widehat\Sigma-\tau I
\preceq
\Sigma.
\]

The lower matrix belongs to the uncertainty set and therefore attains the
minimum robust risk.

This yields an exact interval for the robust risk induced solely by covariance
estimation uncertainty.

At the boundary

\[
\tau=\lambda_{\min}(\widehat\Sigma),
\]

the lower endpoint becomes singular. The upper theorem remains valid, but the
A60 positive-definite ellipsoid formula is interpreted through a limit at the
singular lower endpoint.

## 4. Containment inside the certified error box

The maximum coordinate displacement of an ellipsoid with covariance
\(\Sigma\) is

\[
\sqrt{\Sigma_{ii}}.
\]

For every covariance in the spectral ball,

\[
\Sigma_{ii}
\le
\widehat\Sigma_{ii}+\tau.
\]

Thus a sufficient uniform containment condition is

\[
\widehat\Sigma_{ii}+\tau
\le
\frac1{400}
\qquad
(i=1,2,3).
\]

For the audited estimate, the largest diagonal entry is

\[
\frac9{10000}.
\]

Therefore

\[
\tau
\le
\frac1{400}
-
\frac9{10000}
=
\frac1{625}.
\]

Every covariance in the uncertainty set then produces an ellipsoid contained
in the A58 box \([1,1.1]^3\).

## 5. Quadratic robust root with covariance inflation

A60 gives the exact robust root through

\[
(a-yb)^2
=
(n-yd)^\top
\Sigma
(n-yd).
\]

Under spectral inflation,

\[
\Sigma_\tau
=
\widehat\Sigma+\tau I,
\]

so

\[
(a-yb)^2
=
(n-yd)^\top
\widehat\Sigma
(n-yd)
+
\tau\|n-yd\|_2^2.
\]

The quadratic coefficients become affine functions of \(\tau\):

\[
A(\tau)
=
b^2
-
d^\top\widehat\Sigma d
-
\tau\|d\|_2^2,
\]

\[
B(\tau)
=
-2ab
+
2n^\top\widehat\Sigma d
+
2\tau n^\top d,
\]

\[
C(\tau)
=
a^2
-
n^\top\widehat\Sigma n
-
\tau\|n\|_2^2.
\]

Thus the covariance-estimation adjustment remains algebraic.

## 6. Sensitivity to the spectral radius

Define

\[
F(y,\tau)
=
a-yb
+
\sqrt{
(n-yd)^\top
(\widehat\Sigma+\tau I)
(n-yd)

\]

At the robust root,

\[
F(y_\tau,\tau)=0.
\]

Implicit differentiation gives

\[
\frac{dy_\tau}{d\tau}
=
\frac{
\|v_\tau\|_2^2
}{
2\sqrt{v_\tau^\top\Sigma_\tau v_\tau}

\left[
b+

d^\top\Sigma_\tau v_\tau

\sqrt{v_\tau^\top\Sigma_\tau v_\tau}

where

\[
v_\tau=n-y_\tau d.

This derivative is positive. Passing to the future score,

\[
\frac{dQ}{d\tau}
=
\frac1{2(\log2)y_\tau}

\]

At \(\tau=0\), the audited value is

\[
\lambda_\tau

## 7. Interpretation

A covariance matrix estimated from finitely many calibration repetitions is
not exact. A61 separates two objects:

1. the estimated covariance \(\widehat\Sigma\);
2. a certified spectral uncertainty radius \(\tau\).

Once those are supplied, the worst robust risk is calculated by replacing

\[
\widehat\Sigma
\]

with

\[
\widehat\Sigma+\tau I.
\]

No sampling of covariance matrices is needed.

The inflation \(\tau I\) is not a claim that the true covariance error is
isotropic. It is the Loewner envelope of every symmetric perturbation with
operator norm at most \(\tau\).

## 8. Logical status

### Established

1. The exact worst covariance in a spectral ball is
   \(\widehat\Sigma+\tau I\).
2. The exact best covariance is
   \(\widehat\Sigma-\tau I\) whenever that matrix remains positive definite.
3. The resulting robust-risk interval is algebraic.
4. The containment condition inside the A58 box is explicit.
5. The covariance-uncertainty premium increases monotonically with \(\tau\).
6. The local premium slope is supplied.
7. A machine-readable input template is supplied.

### Not established

1. A61 does not derive \(\tau\) from a sample size.
2. No confidence level is assigned.
3. No bootstrap or concentration method is preferred.
4. The centre and anchor design remain fixed.
5. Heavy-tailed covariance estimation is not addressed.
6. Covariance and mean-estimation errors are not coupled.

---

## 9. Next rigorous target

The covariance-confidence-set theorem is now closed.

The next bridge should estimate \(\tau\) from repeated calibration data while
remaining honest about assumptions. A controlled route is:

1. estimate \(\widehat\Sigma\);
2. use a nonparametric bootstrap to obtain an empirical spectral-radius
   distribution;
3. report a chosen upper quantile \(\widehat\tau_\alpha\);
4. feed
   \[
   \widehat\Sigma+\widehat\tau_\alpha I
   \]
   into A60.

That next step would be a statistical protocol rather than another purely
algebraic theorem.
