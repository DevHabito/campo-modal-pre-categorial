# M1 — Formal Manuscript Split and Claim Freeze

**Project:** Pre-Categorical Modal Field Framework  
**Author:** Felipe Gianini Romero  
**Status:** Frozen editorial architecture after Novelty Audits C1 and C2

## Decision

The research archive is divided into two manuscript programmes with different
readiness states.

### Manuscript C — combinatorics

**Working title**

> Condensation Preorders of Labeled Digraphs: Complete and Representative
> Encodings, Exact Enumeration, and Edge-Toggle Sensitivity

**Frozen thesis**

A loopless labeled digraph determines a complete reflexive reachability
preorder, while the earlier minimum-representative code discards SCC membership
information. The two encodings have different exact counts, different fibers,
and a well-defined edge-toggle sensitivity.

**Readiness**

The definitions, correction, enumerations, and C1 novelty audit are ready.
The manuscript is **not submission-ready** because the edge-toggle result is
currently finite:

\[
P_2=1,\qquad P_3=\frac34,\qquad P_4=\frac12,\qquad
P_5=\frac{75}{256}.
\]

A structural result for \(P_n\)—a decomposition, recurrence, bound, exact
subclass, or asymptotic theorem—is the blocking scientific gate.

### Manuscript F — foundational

**Working title**

> Informational Requirements for Pre-Categorical Relational Models:
> Identifiability, Measure, Dynamic Closure, and Coarse-Graining

**Frozen thesis**

Order alone does not provide the quantitative, measure-theoretic, transition,
and occupancy information required for the operations studied. The current
audits motivate the provisional informational architecture

\[
(R,q,\mu,K,\pi),
\]

with each component tied to a distinct obstruction. The strongest exact
project result is the constructive failure of closure of
\((\bar q,Q_\lambda)\) under the centered contraction.

**Readiness**

The claim set is ready for a first integrated draft. Classical results must be
attributed, and the architecture must be called provisional rather than a
unique or physical ontology.

## Hard separation rules

1. The main manuscripts are organized by definitions and logical dependency,
   not by audit numbers.
2. `Axx` and `C1/C2` appear only in reproducibility notes, supplements, and
   repository links.
3. The combinatorics paper contains no pregeometry, physical time, or gravity
   narrative.
4. The foundational paper contains no claim of deriving spacetime, distance,
   curvature, gravity, matter, or an empirical map for \(q,\mu,\pi\).
5. Noise universality and the geometry track do not enter the foundational
   main text.
6. The classical count \(6942\) is attributed; it is not a novelty claim.
7. MF-R036 and MF-R047 are background/lemmas, MF-R048 is a central exact
   framework corollary, and MF-R049 is the central exact counterexample.
