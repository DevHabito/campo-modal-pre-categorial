# Reviewer Independence and Conflict Policy

An external review counts toward the F4 independence gate only when:

1. the reviewer did not write or materially design the audited proofs;
2. the reviewer did not execute the canonical computational audit whose result
   they are asked to validate;
3. the reviewer has relevant mathematical expertise;
4. conflicts and prior project involvement are disclosed;
5. the reviewer checks the manuscript itself and records a written verdict.

A friendly informal reaction, automated language-model critique, or review by
the author does not close the independent-review gate. Multiple reviewers from
the same close collaboration may provide useful feedback but do not provide
the strongest form of independence.

The repository should preserve the review date, reviewed commit, reviewer
scope, and resolution of every blocking or major comment.
