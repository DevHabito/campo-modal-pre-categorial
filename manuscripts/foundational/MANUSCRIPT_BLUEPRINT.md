# Manuscript F — Foundational Blueprint

## Working title

**Informational Requirements for Pre-Categorical Relational Models:
Identifiability, Measure, Dynamic Closure, and Coarse-Graining**

## One-sentence thesis

The operations studied—cardinal comparison, refinement, transition, static
aggregation, and dynamic coarse-graining—cannot all be supported by order
alone; the current results motivate a provisional informational architecture

\[
(R,q,\mu,K,\pi)
\]

with explicit independence, identifiability, and closure limitations.

## Central original value

The paper's contribution is not a collection of new versions of classical
theorems. It is:

1. an obstruction-driven dependency map;
2. the disciplined separation of order, cardinal marks, additive mass,
   transition law, and occupancy;
3. the exact transport identity for the chosen centered contraction;
4. the exact four-point demonstration that
   \((\bar q,Q_\lambda)\) is not generally dynamically closed;
5. the observable-relative account of static and dynamic coarse-graining.

## Strongest exact proposition

Two finite distributions can have the same mean and the same fixed-\(\lambda\)
effective score but different \(Q_{a\lambda}\), hence different future
\(Q_\lambda\) under the centered contraction.

## Intended audience

Mathematical foundations of relational models, identifiability, stochastic
coarse-graining, measurement theory, applied probability, and formal
pregeometry.

## Required tone

- theorem/corollary/lemma labels must reflect novelty status;
- classical results are attributed;
- the architecture is provisional;
- no physical emergence claim is made.
